Welcome to Mumbai

Catering to a plethora of travellers every year, Mumbai serves a huge platter of activities that are likely to quench your thirst
for adventure, history, culture, food and architecture. With a glorious colonial past serving as a Dutch and British empire
and a tantalising present that attracts people from all over the world to explore and even build a life here, Mumbai has magic that
truly makes it ‘Aamchi’ for us all.
Why we love Mumbai
With a coastline of over 150 km and a city life that oozes with pure goodness of luxury & vivacity, Mumbai brings together
the best of both worlds for every visitor.
The street food & the nightlife of Mumbai are exceptional, and so are the experiences of shopping and street walks through
its landmarks. Topping off with pleasant weather most months, Mumbai is a joyride of a happening holiday.
An Alpha City of the world, Mumbai is a true cosmopolitan with people of different nationalities, cultures, professions all
finding a home here. The culture of Mumbai is a contemporary mix of all these individuals, which makes it all the more
endearing and exotic.
Back in Time
The first railway line, first bus service, and the first airport of India all find their genesis in the city of dreams, telling us that
the city has always been ahead of its time.
Mumbai derives its name from a local deity, called Mumbadevi. Although, until 1995, it was officially called Bombay, a name
given by the Portuguese and the English that roughly translates to ‘good bay’.
Mumbai holds a rich history. Interestingly, there are caves in the city, the oldest Kanheri Caves dating back to 1st century BC!
The Elephanta Caves, reached by ferry through the city, is a popular tourist destination along with being a UNESCO world
heritage site.
Fun Facts
From UNESCO world heritage sites and the world’s most expensive house to Asia’s largest slum, Mumbai is a treasure of
scenes that reflect different ages, cultures, and stories altogether.
Mumbai is known for its impeccable tiffin service system. Spread out across the entirety of the city that houses 1.84 crore
people, the ‘Dabbawalas’ work in such a well-organized way, that their operations are studied by management students in
the country!
Mumbai is home to many a quaint Iranian-style cafes and bakeries called ‘Irani Cafes’, which remain a favourite snack place
for locals till date, so much so, that it is a part of lifestyle for some.
